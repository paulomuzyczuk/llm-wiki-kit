# Gaps

## §1 — Knowledge gaps found in ingested content

- **Open-source legal coverage is principle-level by design.** Fogel's Ch.9 "Legal Matters"
  ([[producing-open-source-software-book]]) is explicitly framed by the author as an
  *introduction* — he defers deep licensing, patent, and trademark law to specialist resources
  (Meeker, Lindberg, the OSI license pages). The vault's legal pages ([[open-source-licensing]],
  [[copyleft]], [[permissive-licensing]], [[license-compatibility]], [[contributor-agreements]],
  [[proprietary-relicensing]], [[trademarks-in-open-source]], [[software-patents]],
  [[creative-commons-licensing]]) therefore capture decision criteria and governing principles,
  not operative legal mechanics. A tier-1 legal-reference source would be needed to deepen this
  area beyond practitioner-guide depth. (Surfaced by the producing-open-source-software ingest,
  batch 17 / book completion.)
